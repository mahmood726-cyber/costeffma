## E156 Body (CURRENT)
We asked whether pooling incremental cost-effectiveness data across economic evaluations yields reliable summary measures of value for money. Six published cost-effectiveness analyses provided incremental costs, incremental effects (QALYs), standard errors, and cost-effect correlations for a common intervention-comparator pair. Net monetary benefit was pooled via DerSimonian-Laird random-effects meta-analysis with Hartung-Knapp-Sidik-Jonkman adjustment at user-specified willingness-to-pay thresholds; ICERs were pooled on the natural-log scale using delta-method standard errors and back-transformed via exponentiation. At WTP = 25,000 GBP, pooled NMB was negative with wide prediction intervals; the cost-effectiveness acceptability curve crossed 50% probability near WTP = 40,000 GBP, suggesting marginal value. Sensitivity analyses across WTP thresholds from 0 to 200,000 GBP confirmed monotonic increase in P(cost-effective) with stable heterogeneity estimates. The tool provides decision-makers with transparent, reproducible cost-effectiveness summaries incorporating uncertainty propagation from individual studies through to acceptability curves. Results are limited to published aggregate data; individual-patient-level cost-effect correlation structure and between-country cost variation remain unmodelled.
